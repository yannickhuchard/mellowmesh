# The Higgs Boson

The Higgs boson is the physical excitation of the Higgs field, which breaks electroweak symmetry and generates mass for quarks, leptons, and the massive W/Z gauge bosons.

## Key Facts

*   **Spin**: 0 (scalar).
*   **Mass**: $\sim 125 \text{ GeV}/c^2$.
*   **Discovery**: Announced by CERN (ATLAS and CMS) in 2012.

Without the Higgs mechanism, W/Z [Bosons](bosons.md) would be massless, and elementary fermions like the electron ([Leptons](leptons.md)) and quarks ([Quarks](quarks.md)) would have no rest mass.
